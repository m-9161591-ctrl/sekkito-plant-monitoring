ANALYTICS

ⓘ About Analytics — click to expand ▸
  • Charts show historical sensor data stored in Firebase Realtime Database.
  • Select a time range above — data is loaded fresh each time you switch.
  • Data is written by the ESP32 sensor node every ~40 seconds to Firebase.
  • If charts are empty, the sensor node may not have written data in that period yet.
  • Water Temperature is stored as water_temperature in Firebase and is shown in a separate chart.

ⓘ How to use Camera Hub — click to expand ▸
  1. Click Capture — the ESP32-CAM takes a photo and sends it here over MQTT.
  2. Once the image appears, click Save Image to download it to your device.
  3. Click Fast AI Analysis to run a quick TFLite classification on the Raspberry Pi — no internet needed.
  4. For a full diagnosis with treatment advice: go to AI Portal, copy the prompt, open Gemini or ChatGPT, upload your saved image, and paste the prompt.
  5. If the camera is offline, the status badge will say "Connection failed" — check the ESP32-CAM is powered on.

AI DIAGNOSIS PORTAL

ⓘ How to get a full AI diagnosis — click to expand ▸
  1. Go to Camera Hub, capture a clear photo of your plant, then click Save Image.
  2. Fill in the plant name, growth stage, and any visible symptoms below.
  3. Click Copy AI Prompt — it includes all live sensor readings automatically.
  4. Click Open Gemini or Open ChatGPT to launch the AI chat.
  5. In the AI chat: upload your saved plant photo, then paste the copied prompt.
  6. The AI will return a diagnosis, treatment steps, and prevention tips.

💡 Tip: Gemini is recommended — it handles plant images very well.
